how the gas emissions related to human health.(headline) :

Findings indicate that emissions from food waste, such as hydrogen sulphide, ammonia, and volatile organic carbons, can affect human endocrine, respiratory, nervous, and olfactory systems. The severity of the human health effects depends on the gaseous concentration, but range from mild lung irritation to cancer and death..

Emissions from food waste can negatively impact human health directly and indirectly. These impacts may include an increasing number of respiratory issues, and mild and severe headaches .

Metaphorically, if food waste were a country, it would be the third-largest greenhouse gas emitting country

How Food Waste Pollutes Our Oceans And Seas:

Food waste is a major global issue. Every year, vast quantities of food are thrown away, and many don't realize the impact this has on our oceans and seas. Even food waste disposed of on land can eventually reach waterways through landfills or runoff.

As food waste decomposes in water, it consumes oxygen that marine life needs to survive. This creates "dead zones" devoid of oxygen, harming fish, plants, and other organisms.

Furthermore, food waste can contribute to excess nutrients in the water, leading to harmful algal blooms. These blooms form layers that block sunlight from reaching plants below, disrupting the entire ecosystem.

Thousands of dollars are thrown away because of food waste

Food waste is a significant issue that impacts businesses across various industries, particularly in the food sector. Owners of restaurants, cafes, and other food-related businesses often struggle with managing their inventory to minimize waste.

Every year, thousands of dollars are thrown away due to food spoilage, overproduction, and improper storage practices.

This not only affects the bottom line of the business but also contributes to environmental problems, such as greenhouse gas emissions from decomposing food waste in landfills.

Ultimately, by taking some steps, owners can not only save money but also contribute to a more sustainable and environmentally friendly operation.
